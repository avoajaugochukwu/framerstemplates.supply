Introduction: The Question Everyone AsksIf you’ve heard about Framer and are wondering, "Is Framer free?"—you’re not alone! Framer is a popular tool for designing and building websites, and many people are curious about its pricing. Maybe you’re looking for a free website builder, or perhaps you want to test the waters before committing to a paid plan. Either way, I’ve got you covered!In this guide, we’ll break down everything you need to know about Framer’s pricing, what you can get for free, and whether upgrading is worth it. For an even more detailed look at every tier, see our [full breakdown of Framer pricing plans](/blog/navigating-framer-pricing-plans-a-guide-from-free-to-pro). And don’t worry—I’ll keep things simple and jargon-free!What is Framer? (For Absolute Beginners)Before we dive into pricing, let’s quickly go over what Framer actually is.Framer is a website design and prototyping tool that lets you create stunning, interactive websites—without coding! Think of it as a more powerful, design-focused alternative to tools like Wix or Squarespace. If you're brand new to the platform, our [beginner's guide to Framer](/blog/the-beginner-s-guide-to-framer-a-simple-way-to-design-stunning-websites) walks you through everything step by step.Whether you’re a designer, marketer, or entrepreneur, Framer helps you build and publish modern websites with ease. It’s especially loved by UI/UX designers because it offers tons of customization options, animations, and interactive elements.Now that we know what Framer is, let’s answer the big question…Is Framer Really Free? (Yes, But With Limits)Yes! Framer does have a free plan, which is great for trying it out. But, like most free plans, it comes with some limitations. Here’s what you get for free:✅ Basic Website Builder – You can design and publish one website using Framer’s drag-and-drop tools.✅ Framer Subdomain – Your site will be hosted on a free Framer subdomain (e.g., yoursite.framer.website).✅ Limited Storage & Bandwidth – Enough for small projects but may not be ideal for larger websites.✅ Framer Branding – Your free site will have a small Framer watermark, which might not look professional.✅ Basic Interactions & Animations – You can still add cool effects and animations, but some advanced features are locked.In short, Framer’s free plan is great for personal projects, testing the platform, or learning the ropes. But if you want a custom domain (e.g., yoursite.com) or advanced features, you’ll need to upgrade.Framer's Paid Plans: What Do You Get?If you want more features and control, Framer offers several paid plans. Who is Framer Free Plan Best For?Now that we’ve covered the pricing, let’s answer an important question: Do you really need a paid plan?💰 Stick to the Free Plan if:You’re just playing around or learning Framer.You need a simple, personal website with no custom domain.You don’t mind the Framer branding.🚀 Upgrade to a Paid Plan if:You want a custom domain and a professional look.You need more storage and performance for a growing website.You’re running a business or using the site for clients.Final Thoughts: Is Framer Free Enough for You?Framer does offer a free plan, but like most free website builders, it comes with trade-offs. If you’re just getting started, Framer Free is a fantastic way to explore the platform and see if it’s the right fit for you.However, if you’re serious about building a professional site, running a business, or using Framer for client work, investing in a paid plan is definitely worth it.
