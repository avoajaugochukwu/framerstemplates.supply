# Framer Portfolio Templates for Developers: How to Highlight Your Coding Skills

As a developer, having a strong online portfolio is crucial for showcasing your technical skills, projects, and expertise. Whether you're a front-end, back-end, or full-stack developer, a well-structured portfolio can help attract potential employers, freelance clients, or collaborators. Framer provides sleek and modern portfolio templates that allow developers to highlight their coding expertise with minimal effort and maximum impact.

In this guide, we'll explore the best Framer portfolio templates for developers and provide tips on how to optimize your portfolio to highlight your skills, experience, and projects effectively. ## Customization Tips for Developer Portfolios

### 1. Include Live Demos of Projects

Add embedded interactive previews of your web applications to give visitors a hands-on experience.

### 2. Integrate GitHub Contributions

Use APIs to showcase GitHub contributions and active repositories in real time.

### 3. Write Technical Blog Posts

Use the CMS feature to write blog posts about coding best practices, problem-solving techniques, and tutorials.

### 4. Use Dark & Light Mode

Offer a toggle switch for visitors to switch between light and dark themes.

### 5. Optimize for SEO

Include meta descriptions, structured data, and fast-loading images to improve your site's visibility.

### 6. Use Code Snippets for Emphasis

Highlight snippets of your best code in a syntax-highlighted format to demonstrate your coding style.

### 7. Add Contact & Inquiry Forms

Make it easy for potential employers or clients to reach out directly through a contact form.

## Conclusion

A well-crafted developer portfolio can make all the difference in attracting the right job offers or freelance clients. By using Framer portfolio templates, you can build a modern, responsive, and highly customizable site that effectively showcases your coding skills.
