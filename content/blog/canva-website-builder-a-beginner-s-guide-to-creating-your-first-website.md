## What Is the Canva Website Builder & Who Is It For?

The Canva website builder is a visual tool designed for non-tech users to create professional-looking websites in just minutes. It works entirely online with drag-and-drop controls—no coding, plugins, or downloads required.

If you've ever designed a social media post or flyer on Canva, you already have the skills to build a website with it.

### Why Beginners Love It:

- Easy drag-and-drop interface
- Templates for every use case (events, portfolios, resumes, businesses)
- Free and mobile-friendly
- Publish instantly using Canva's free hosting

It's like a website-shaped coloring book—you fill it in, and Canva brings it to life online.

**Real Example:** Sarah, a self-taught artist, used Canva to create a portfolio of her watercolor paintings. She chose a clean gallery template, uploaded her images, and went live in under 45 minutes—all without touching code.

### Summary:

- Ideal for creatives, small business owners, students
- No prior tech or design skills needed
- Works great for simple websites and one-pagers

**Reflective question:** What kind of website would help you tell your story today?

## How to Make a Website on Canva (Step-by-Step)

Ready to launch your first website? Here's how to get started using Canva's website builder:

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. Sign in or create an account at canva.com
2. In the search bar, type "Website"
3. Choose a template that fits your project
4. Drag, drop, and edit elements (text, images, buttons)
5. Add extra pages if needed
6. Click "Publish Website"
7. Share your live link (use Canva's free domain or connect your own)

**Beginner Scenario:** Daniel, a teacher, created a school play announcement site. He used a Canva event template, added key details, RSVP buttons, and photos—and had it published before lunch.

It's like building a PowerPoint—but instead of presenting it, you publish it for the world.

### Don't Make These Mistakes:

- Don't overload your first site with 10 pages
- Keep your message clear and layout simple
- Always preview your site on mobile before publishing

## Canva vs Wix: Which Website Builder Should You Choose?

Both Canva and Wix offer website-building tools—but they serve different needs.

| Feature | Canva | Wix |
|---|---|---|
| Ease of Use | Super beginner-friendly | Intermediate |
| Design Flexibility | Visual, fast | Deep customization |
| Blogging Tools | Basic | Strong |
| SEO Tools | Minimal | Robust |
| Best For | Portfolios, one-pagers, resumes | Full-featured websites |

**Real User Story:** Kemi, a photographer, tested both platforms. Canva let her go live in a day. Wix offered more depth but took more time. She stuck with Canva for simplicity and visual control.

Canva is like Google Docs for websites—Wix is like Microsoft Word with a thousand features.

## Common Beginner Mistakes with Canva Websites

Even with a simple tool like Canva, beginners can slip up. Avoid these:

### Common Pitfalls:

- **Overthinking the design** – Keep it simple and clean
- **Skipping mobile view** – Always test how it looks on phones
- **Publishing before proofreading** – Typos kill credibility
- **Not setting a clear goal** – Every page should have one focus

### Do This Instead:

- Start with one page, one goal
- Use Canva's font and layout guides
- Preview on both desktop and mobile
- Test your site with a friend before sharing
